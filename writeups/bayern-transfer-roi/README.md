# Bayern Munich Transfers as an Investment Portfolio

This analysis reframes ten years of Bayern Munich signings (2015/16 through 2025/26) as a finance portfolio: fee paid is the purchase price, and the peak market value the player reached at Bayern is the highest price they could have been sold at. Across 35 paid signings, 27 were profitable on that basis, and the biggest returns came from unheralded names (Gnabry ~11x, Kimmich 9.5x, Davies 5.7x, Bischof a fee-inflated 133x) rather than the marquee buys. The framing angle is that big fees do not guarantee big returns, that free transfers and young signings drove much of the value, and that selling every signing at peak would have netted around 870m euros. Every figure was triple-checked against a Transfermarkt-based workbook before posting.

## The LinkedIn post

With the Champions League final just finishing up and the World Cup approaching, I decided to analyze one of my favourite teams' transfers from the past 10 years: FC Bayern München's signings from 2015/16 through 2025/26.

Thinking about football through a finance lens, I framed Bayern's transfers as a portfolio. Fee paid = purchase price. Peak market value reached at Bayern = the highest price at which you could sell.

What stood out:
- 27 of the 35 paid signings were profitable.
- The biggest winners are not necessarily the biggest names. Serge Gnabry (€8m from Werder, peaked at €90m) returned over 11x. Joshua Kimmich (€9.5m from Stuttgart, peaked at €90m) returned 9.5x. Alphonso Davies (€14m, peaked at €80m) returned 5.7x. Tom Bischof technically tops the list at 133x (€0.3m fee from Hoffenheim, now worth €40m).
- The biggest absolute gain is Michael Olise. €53m from Crystal Palace, now valued at €150m. That is +€97m in under two years.
- Free transfers contributed too. Leon Goretzka peaked at €70m, Konrad Laimer is worth €32m today, Noussair Mazraoui was sold to Man United for €20m. All three joined for €0. (Free transfers are only on the second chart, not the scatter.)
- Big fees do not guarantee big returns. Lucas Hernández (€80m) peaked at €70m and was sold to PSG for €45m. Sacha Boey (€30m) is worth €15m today. Sadio Mané and Matthijs de Ligt returned modest multiples before being sold.

A few limitations:
- Market value is not cash. It ignores wages, bonuses, agent fees, and sporting value (trophies, minutes, goals).
- Academy graduates like Lennart Karl and Jamal Musiala are not included since they were not transfer signings.
- Peak value flatters players whose form dropped later.

Bayern's best deals were noticeably young players. Tel signed at 17, Davies at 18, Bischof at 19, Kimmich at 20, Gnabry at 21.

If Bayern sold every player at peak market value, they could have earned €870m!

Do you think any other club has done as well as Bayern on transfers in the past decade?
