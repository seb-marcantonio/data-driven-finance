# Trump's April 8 Trades and the Tariff-Pause Rally

A close read of the 327 stocks Donald Trump bought on April 8, 2025, the day before he paused his Liberation Day tariffs and the market jumped 9.5%. Drawn from his 2025 federal disclosure (21,285 trades total for the year), the post shows that while the April 8 basket beat the S&P 500 (roughly 75% vs 52% held to July 2026), the outperformance collapses to about index-level once SanDisk is removed, and the median pick actually trailed the market. The angle is deliberately deflationary: his sharpest-looking trade of the year was average once one lucky stock is stripped out, and it says nothing about his full-year record.

## The LinkedIn post

Donald Trump made 21,285 trades in one year (2025). That is about 85 every trading day. 327 of them landed the day before he paused his Liberation Day tariffs, and the market jumped 9.5%.

Put 327 in perspective. His 327 buys on April 8 ran more than four times his usual, and only 10 of his 134 buying days all year were heavier. When the market jumped the next day, those 327 stocks rose about 9% with it, a gain of roughly $730,000 on what he bought.

His single biggest buying day was not April 8. It was March 5, when he bought 640 stocks, nearly double. The market then fell 1.8% the next day and 4.2% over the next week. His heaviest buying was badly timed. April 8 stood out only because of the decision he made the next day, not because he can pick a bottom.

As for those 327, held from that day to July 2026 they are up about 75%, versus 52% for the S&P 500. As a group, they beat the market.

However, if you take out SanDisk, the biggest earner, the 326 returned about 54%, roughly the same as the index. Most of the individual picks actually trailed the market: the median returned 39%, and only 40% of them beat the S&P.

This is only those 327 trades from a single day, not his full year, so it says nothing about his overall record. But the batch that looks most like a call on the tariff pause beat the market on the back of one lucky stock, not a broad winning bet.

Strip out one stock and his sharpest-looking trade of the year was just average. Skill, or SanDisk?

Methodology

The trades come from Trump's 2025 federal financial disclosure (OGE Form 278e). I parsed the full filing into 21,285 individual transactions and isolated the 327 buys dated April 8, 2025.

Returns run from each stock's April 8 close to its July 2026 close, using daily closing prices. The group figures (about 75% with SanDisk, 54% without) are equal-weighted averages of the per-stock returns, so every position counts the same regardless of size. The median is the middle stock. The S&P 500 comparison covers the same window, and "beat the market" means a stock topped the index over that period.

Two caveats. A disclosure shows what was traded, not who chose the trade, so these may be manager decisions rather than his own calls. And the forms report dollar amounts in ranges, so the roughly $730,000 gain is an estimate built from range midpoints, not an exact figure.
