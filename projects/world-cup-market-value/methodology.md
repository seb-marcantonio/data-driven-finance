# Methodology: "How much does a great World Cup boost your market value?"

## Objective
Measure how a strong World Cup changes a footballer's market value at two levels, individual players and whole national-team squads, and frame the tournament as a re-rating (earnings) event.

## Source and definitions
All values are Transfermarkt market values, in euros (EUR). A Transfermarkt market value is a community and editorial estimate of a player's transfer worth, not an actual sale price. It is updated in periodic batches, including a dedicated update after major tournaments.

- **Before**: the last market value dated before the tournament kicked off.
- **After**: the first market value dated after the tournament, i.e. the post-tournament revaluation.

Tournament reference dates: 2014 kickoff 12 Jun, final 13 Jul; 2018 kickoff 14 Jun, final 15 Jul; 2022 kickoff 20 Nov, final 18 Dec.

## Individual players
Seven standout players from 2014, 2018, and 2022 were selected to span positions (goalkeeper, defender, midfield, attack) and a range of profiles, from unknowns to established stars. Before and after values were read directly from each player's Transfermarkt "Market value over time" history, taking the last pre-tournament entry and the first post-tournament entry. Percentage change is (after − before) / before.

## Transfer fees
Where a player moved after the tournament, the fee is included as the "cash-out." Each fee was cross-checked against at least one major outlet (Sky Sports, ESPN, Forbes, France24) in addition to Transfermarkt's transfer record. Fees originally reported in GBP are converted to EUR at the rate around the deal date, with both noted (for example, Enzo Fernández £106.8m is about €121m). James Rodríguez's fee was never officially disclosed; reported figures range roughly €75m to €80m, shown here as "around €75m."

## Team squad values
For each tournament's final four (semifinalists), the total squad market value is the sum of that tournament's squad on Transfermarkt.

- **2022**: both before and after read directly from archived Transfermarkt participants pages (16 Nov 2022, and the 1 Jan 2023 snapshot that captures the 23 Dec 2022 revaluation).
- **2018**: before read from the archived participants page (14 Jun 2018); after reconstructed by summing each player's individual market value at the 16 Jul 2018 revaluation.
- **2014**: both totals reconstructed by summing individual player values, at 14 Jun 2014 (before) and 22 Jul 2014 (after), because no archived participants page captured the 2014 revaluation on the same squad. As a check, the reconstructed before totals matched the archived 14 Jun 2014 participants page for Argentina, Netherlands, and Brazil, confirming the roster and value extraction.

Percentage change is computed on the totals.

## Note on Germany 2014
Germany 2014 carries a caveat. Its provisional pre-tournament squad (€562m) included Marco Reus, who withdrew injured and was replaced by Shkodran Mustafi. On the actual 23-man squad that played, the total is €538m before and €569.5m after, a +5.9% rise, which is the figure used.

## Currency and rounding
Everything is standardized to EUR, Transfermarkt's native currency. Third-party trackers quoting GBP or USD (for example GOAL/eToro or beIN) were not mixed into these figures. France's 2022 squad total is shown to three significant figures (Transfermarkt displayed roughly €1.00bn before and €1.06bn after).

## Verification
Every figure was taken from Transfermarkt (player value-history pages or archived squad pages) and, for transfers, cross-checked against a second reputable source. All percentages were recomputed from the displayed before and after values.

## Limitations
- Market value is an estimate, not a transaction price.
- Transfermarkt updates in batches, so "before" and "after" are the nearest updates around the tournament, not the exact kickoff or final dates.
- The 2018 team "after" may slightly undercount Croatia and England: a few players (Modrić, Rakitić, Kane, De Bruyne) were not repriced until the October 2018 update, so those squad gains are conservative.
- Team totals for 2014 and 2018 are reconstructed by summing individual player values rather than read off a single official post-tournament page. The reconstruction was validated against the archived 2014 pre-tournament page (see above).
- Pre-tournament squad totals are mildly sensitive to the sampling date; some outlets quote marginally different numbers.

## 2026 note
Transfermarkt's official post-2026 revaluation was expected around 22 July 2026, a few days after the 19 July final. The 2026 names in the post (Yamal, Cubarsí, Bouaddi) are qualitative until those figures publish, at which point the charts can be refreshed with confirmed values.
